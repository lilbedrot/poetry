Doing laps of the Milky Way

(A collection of poems by yaeba girl)

Oceania

With the jellyfish,
I was floating-
Just thinking of you

Apocalypse

Specks of stardust glitter around us
And ignite as our lips meet
Falling in low gravity
Smoldering at our feet
We orbit eachother
Endless and complete
Time dissolves into nothingness
Our hearts weightless and sweet

Valency

I wish to drown a little
In shallow people-
Brew the storm in their teacup

Gumtrees

A quietly glowing ball of light can settle and be
While softly burning in the eloquent fashion of soft grey bark-
The cool touch of taupe green leaves
Love smoothly from the tones of a sultry sunset of dusked red on their undersides.
Leaves hum loudly on the branches that move along the swell of the tides;
As if a pulse could be seen and unheard in the most literal mind,
But in the most unliteral of senses it fills the air-
Everything becomes aware of the very grids life is assembled upon,
No one thing can feel even a small and meer part of itself
And not feel its opposite pulling in a different direction

Golden

With or without you, I ignite.
In spaces you don't speak their hides sweet surreal redolence-
A hunt to kiss enigma;
An intense melodious rush.

I am your wounded auxiliary function
Underneath you my lacerations weep-
Mellifluous, through your fingers,
Pooling liquid gold at your front door.

Bruise

Loneliness
Is a kind of soft bleed,
Of wounds with depths that reach a rudimentary core;
A universally congenital ache,
To which we are born.

Fool

Pre-emptive, egocentric bombs you placed in my hands,
Thin-skinned black cherries settling and unsettling in a daze of invisible hues,
With just enough room to sigh

At nine, I sit and wait for you with handfuls of cherries resting on my thighs,
And a thin veil of sweat collects in the vallies of my palm creases-
The sweaty, sticky mess resonates with pangs of follishness.
